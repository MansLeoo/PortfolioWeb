Chal'enge Challenge Unity VR Training

VR'Tech

Virtual reality training solution dedicated to industrial quality control. Developed for Vertech', the application immerses new operators in the glass production process.

Read Presentation
The Chal'enge Challenge

A 15-day academic project bringing together students from Arts et Métiers, CNAM BFC (Computer Science Bachelor's), and EGC Centre-Est (Marketing Bachelor's). The goal is to combine various technical (VR, AI) and strategic expertise to design functional prototypes addressing companies' needs.

Vertech'

World-leading software provider of Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES) dedicated to the glass industry. Based in Chalon-sur-Saône, the company equips over 800 production lines internationally and continuously innovates to optimize quality, traceability, and efficiency of manufacturing lines through Industry 4.0 solutions.

Context and Objectives

This project was carried out as part of the Chal'enge Challenge (an intensive 15-day project). Our multidisciplinary team of 8 members (two Master 2 students, one Master 1 student, one 3rd-year Bachelor student, two 1st-year IT Bachelor students, and two Marketing Bachelor students) chose to address the issue submitted by Vertech. Throughout the development, we worked closely with a technical referent from the company.

Vertech's objective was to experiment with Virtual Reality to showcase a glass production line and train operators on two distinct quality control stations:

  • "Hot End" control: The user must manipulate virtual tongs to retrieve an incandescent bottle coming out of the machine and precisely weigh it on a scale.
  • "Cold End" control: The user must manually grab a cooled bottle and inspect it from all angles to ensure it has no defects (scratches, fractures).

The Developed Application

In practice, VR'Tech immerses the learner in the heart of a glass production plant. Equipped with a Meta Quest 3 headset, the user is guided through the logistics line, observing the continuous path of bottles on the conveyors. To accompany this discovery, interactive information bubbles line the route to present and explain each key stage of the production line.

The educational experience is based on two interactive phases: First at the "Hot End" station, where the operator must use virtual tongs with precision to extract an incandescent bottle and perform a control weighing. Then, at the "Cold End" station, where the user manually handles cooled bottles for a meticulous visual examination to detect the slightest anomaly (scratches, micro-fractures).

Technical Challenges

1. Logistics Flow System

Design of a precise navigation system for bottles on conveyors, including intelligent routing to various inspection machines based on defect probabilities.

2. Optimization and Preparation of 3D Assets

Cleaning and slicing of complex CAD models provided by the company to enable the animation of mechanical components and ensure fluidity on the Meta Quest 3.

My Role and Contributions

  • Unity Development (C#): Bottle movement logic via a waypoint system, management of routing and defect probabilities.
  • User Interactions: Development of gripping and inspection mechanics for bottles, as well as the integration of contextual interfaces.
  • 3D Modeling and Optimization: Cleaning of CAD models, animation of mechanical components in 3ds Max, and optimization for the Meta Quest 3.
