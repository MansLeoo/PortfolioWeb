Hackathon Unity VR Accessibility

Franco-German Hackathon

Awareness application for various visual impairments (color blindness, astigmatism). Project realized in 3 days in international collaboration to promote digital accessibility.

Context and Objectives

This project was carried out as part of an intensive 3-day Franco-German hackathon, bringing together students from Arts et Métiers and the German university HAW Hamburg. The main objective of this event was to design and present a virtual reality application aimed at raising public awareness of various disability situations.

Divided into groups of 4 students, we chose to focus our experience on visual impairments (color blindness and astigmatism).

The Experience: A Virtual Museum

To offer an approach that is both fun and educational, we decided to design the application as a virtual museum.

The tour begins with an exhibition gallery showcasing artworks created by artists with color blindness. Through an interactive VR interface, the user can select the specific type of color blindness they wish to simulate. The visual environment then adapts in real-time, allowing the user to understand the altered color perception of these artists.

The journey then continues into a room entirely dedicated to astigmatism. Since this condition is notably characterized by a directional distortion of light sources, we designed a dark environment to highlight these effects.

The room incorporates highly visual elements: urban traffic lights, a car with its headlights on, as well as neon signs. By activating the simulation, the user experiences the stretching of lights, the "halo" effect, and glare, strikingly reproducing the visual difficulties of an astigmatic person, especially at night.

My Role in the Project

Within the team, I was specifically in charge of developing the astigmatism simulation, in order to faithfully reproduce the daily visual disturbances (blur, glare, light distortion).

  • Level Design: Creation of the dark room and arrangement of strategic light sources (neons, headlights, traffic lights).
  • Visual Programming: Implementation of optical distortion and halo effects around lights.
  • User Experience: Programming interactions to activate the simulation in the VR environment.

Technical Challenges

1. Accurate Visual Rendering via Post-Processing

The major challenge was to simulate astigmatism realistically and optimally in Unity. I chose not to use heavy shaders, but rather to leverage Unity's Post-Processing stack combined with the targeted addition of Lens Flares. This approach faithfully reproduced the characteristic stretching of light sources perceived by an astigmatic eye.

2. VR Performance (Meta Quest 3)

The application had to run smoothly on a standalone headset. The smart use of native Post-Processing rather than custom solutions maintained a high refresh rate (72/90 FPS), thus avoiding motion sickness while ensuring a convincing visual render.

3. International Teamwork

Intensive 3-day collaboration with a team of 4 French and German-speaking developers. This involved rigorous version control with Git and quick coordination to integrate the different modules (color blindness, myopia, astigmatism) into a single, cohesive VR experience.
