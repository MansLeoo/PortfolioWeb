Internship Unity VR Mathematics WebXR

Mathematical Escape Game

Virtual reality application for linear algebra revision.

Laboratoire de Physique de Clermont (LPC)

The Laboratoire de Physique de Clermont (LPC) is a joint research unit (UMR 6533) affiliated with the CNRS (IN2P3) and Clermont Auvergne University. Historically focused on particle physics, it also conducts cutting-edge interdisciplinary research applied to the fields of health, environment, and energy.

Context and objectives

This project is part of my first year of Master's degree and was conducted in close collaboration with the Laboratoire de Physique de Clermont (LPC).

The objective of this internship was to develop an immersive and playful virtual reality application. It is intended to support first-year students encountering learning difficulties in mathematics. To meet this need for an innovative educational medium, the idea of a virtual reality "Escape Game" was conceived.

The developed application

The user is immersed in an environment simulating a vast mathematics laboratory. To escape the building as quickly as possible, they must solve a series of puzzles, each calling upon specific mathematical concepts.

Among the various notions covered during this experience, the student is led to mobilize their knowledge of set theory, vector calculus, and matrix calculus.

To ensure maximum dissemination among students, the project was designed to be accessible to the greatest number. In addition to the version designed for virtual reality headsets, particular attention was paid to cross-platform accessibility.

The application exploits WebXR technologies, allowing users without VR headsets to explore the Escape Game directly from their standard web browser, thus ensuring educational equity.

Technical challenges

1. Integrating VR into puzzle mechanics

The first major challenge was to find a relevant conceptual way to integrate interactions specific to virtual reality into the core of mathematical puzzle-solving, ensuring that VR brings real added value to the learning process.

2. Hybrid playability: VR and PC (Keyboard/Mouse)

It was necessary to structure the code and inputs to ensure that the application is fully playable and ergonomic using a VR headset, but also with traditional keyboard and mouse controls.

3. Optimization and LOD management

A drastic optimization of the application was necessary. The implementation of LOD (Level of Detail) scripts, dynamically hiding certain distant objects, allows the application to run smoothly on WebXR and across a wide range of computers.
