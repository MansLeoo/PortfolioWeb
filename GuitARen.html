Applied Research Unity AR / Passthrough Music

Guit'AR

Augmented reality application designed to facilitate intuitive learning of guitar scales using hand tracking.

Conservatoire du Grand Chalon

Arts education institution. This project was conducted in direct collaboration with the Conservatory to design an innovative educational tool, conceived by and for musicians.

Context and objectives

This project is part of a Master's program and was carried out in close collaboration with the Conservatoire de Musique du Grand Chalon, specifically with professor-researcher Mr. Hackerman.

The objective of this applied research was to experiment with augmented reality as a new educational tool for learning guitar scales, overcoming the limitations of traditional paper sheet music.

The developed application

An augmented reality application was developed, allowing users to visualize scale diagrams and move them freely within their field of view. This represents a major ergonomic advantage compared to a simple sheet of paper on a music stand.

Furthermore, to facilitate visual learning, these scales can be displayed directly on a 3D virtual guitar. Users can thus see the exact finger placement in a "mirror" view and reproduce it on their own guitar. For practical and immersive reasons, the application uses hand tracking, completely freeing the musician from VR controllers.

The initial objective also included real-time tracking of a physical guitar to superimpose notes directly onto it. Tests were conducted using QR codes placed on the instrument, but were not entirely conclusive within the allotted time.

Finally, this experimentation highlighted certain hardware limitations for this specific use case. Challenges related to Passthrough camera resolution for reading fine details, as well as the slight latency perceived during fast playing movements, were notably documented.

Technical challenges

1. Spatial interface and hand tracking

Design of a user interface intuitively manipulable with hands, such as pinching to grab or swiping to move. The user interacts with floating panels containing the scales, without ever needing to let go of the guitar to grab a controller.

2. Instrument tracking prototyping

Research and implementation of solutions for tracking a physical guitar in space. Several approaches were experimented with, with and without the use of QR codes and fiducial markers, attempting to anchor holograms directly onto the neck of the moving instrument.
